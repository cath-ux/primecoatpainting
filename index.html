Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common questions about hiring a painter in New Orleans.

The cost to hire a painter in New Orleans typically ranges from $2 to $6 per square foot depending on the type of work, surface prep required, and paint quality. Prime Coat Painting offers free, no-obligation estimates so you know exactly what to expect before any work begins.

We serve all of New Orleans including the French Quarter, Garden District, Uptown, Lakeview, Mid-City, Gentilly, and Algiers. We also serve the surrounding communities of Metairie, Kenner, Chalmette, and Slidell.

Yes. Prime Coat Painting is fully licensed and insured in the state of Louisiana. We carry comprehensive liability insurance and workers compensation. All of our painters are trained, background-checked professionals.

A single-room interior painting project typically takes 1 day. A full interior repaint of a standard New Orleans home takes 2 to 4 days. Exterior painting usually takes 2 to 5 days depending on the size of the property and weather conditions. We provide a detailed timeline before starting every job.

Louisiana's heat and humidity demand paints with mold and mildew resistance. For exteriors, we recommend 100% acrylic latex paints with UV protection. For interiors, low-VOC semi-gloss or satin finishes work best in bathrooms and kitchens. We select the right products for each surface and room based on years of experience in the New Orleans climate.

Yes. We have extensive experience with the historic architecture throughout New Orleans, including Creole cottages, shotgun homes, camelback houses, and Victorian properties. We understand the importance of preserving original wood, millwork, and architectural details while delivering a fresh, lasting finish.

Yes. We provide commercial painting for offices, retail stores, restaurants, hotels, and multi-unit properties across New Orleans. We schedule work around your business hours to minimize disruption, including nights and weekends when needed.

Yes. All of our painting projects come with a comprehensive workmanship warranty. If any issues arise from our work - peeling, cracking, or uneven coverage - we will return and fix it at no charge. We stand fully behind the quality of every job we complete.

The best time for exterior painting in New Orleans is during the fall and spring months - October through November and March through May - when temperatures and humidity levels are more moderate. Summer heat and humidity can affect paint adhesion and drying times. We monitor weather conditions carefully and schedule exterior jobs on optimal days year-round.
